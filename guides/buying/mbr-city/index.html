Should You Buy in Mohammed Bin Rashid City?

An honest guide to buying property in Mohammed Bin Rashid City, Dubai — including the renovation costs nobody tells you about.

Quick Facts

Developer: Meydan / Sobha / District One
Villa Types: 4BR to 7BR luxury villas and mansions
Age: 2017–2025, mix of established and new
Active Listings: 936+
Renovation Budget: AED 200,000–2,000,000

The Community

A mega-development anchored by District One, Sobha Hartland, and Meydan. Home to the Crystal Lagoon, Dubai's largest man-made lagoon, with contemporary mansions offering direct lagoon access. A premium address attracting HNWI buyers who expect finishes well beyond developer standard. The community combines urban connectivity with resort-style living.

Renovation Reality Check

Investing in MBR City? At these price points, post-handover renovation is expected. Budget AED 200K-2M to match finishes to the address.

Based on our experience working in Mohammed Bin Rashid City, here are the most common renovation needs:

Developer finishes below buyer expectations

At AED 10M+ price points, buyers expect Italian marble and designer fixtures — not the standard porcelain and chrome delivered. Complete bathroom and kitchen overhauls are standard post-handover.

Smart home integration gaps

Despite premium pricing, many villas lack integrated automation. Lutron lighting, Crestron AV, automated blinds and whole-house control systems are typical upgrade requests.

Landscape and pool upgrades

Many plots include large pools with basic finishes. Infinity edge conversions, glass-walled features, and resort-style landscaping match the luxury positioning.

Privacy and security enhancements

Boundary wall upgrades, privacy screening, automated gates, and CCTV integration for residents who require enhanced security.

What We'd Budget

For a typical villa in Mohammed Bin Rashid City, renovation costs range from AED 200,000–2,000,000 depending on scope. Kitchen-only renovations start lower, while full villa transformations with pool and landscaping sit at the top end.
