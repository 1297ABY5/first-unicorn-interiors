How Much Does a Bathroom Renovation Cost in Dubai?

A single bathroom renovation in Dubai costs between AED 15,000 and AED 80,000 depending on size, finishes, and scope. Most villa bathrooms fall in the AED 20,000 to AED 40,000 range for a complete gut-and-refit including demolition, waterproofing, tiling, sanitaryware, vanity, shower, and fixtures.

Multiple Bathrooms? Here's the Real Saving

Most Dubai villas have 3-5 bathrooms. Renovating multiple bathrooms together saves 15-25% compared to doing them one at a time because of bulk material purchasing and shared labour mobilisation.

Pro Tip: Waterproofing is non-negotiable. A failed waterproofing membrane can cause AED 50,000+ in damage to floors below. We apply double-layer waterproofing with a 48-hour flood test on every bathroom — certified and photographed before tiling begins.

Timeline: How Long Per Bathroom?

A single bathroom takes 10 to 14 working days from demolition to handover. When doing multiple bathrooms simultaneously, the total timeline reduces significantly because work overlaps. Three bathrooms done together: approximately 3-4 weeks total, not 6-9 weeks sequentially.

Common Mistakes That Cost You Money

  • Skipping waterproofing — saves AED 3,000 now, costs AED 50,000 later when your downstairs ceiling collapses
  • Choosing tiles before measuring — leads to excessive cutting and waste, adding 15-20% to tile costs
  • Separate contractor for glass — shower glass should be measured after tiling is complete, not before. Integrated teams avoid this coordination failure
  • Ignoring ventilation — Dubai humidity destroys bathrooms without proper exhaust. Budget AED 500-1,500 for a quality fan
