Complete cost breakdown for building a new swimming pool at your Dubai villa. Concrete, fiberglass, overflow, infinity — every option priced transparently.

How Much Does It Cost to Build a Pool in Dubai?

A new swimming pool for a Dubai villa costs between AED 80,000 and AED 500,000+ depending on size, type, and features. The most common residential pool (6m x 3m concrete with mosaic tiling) costs approximately AED 120,000 to AED 180,000 fully installed including permits, filtration, and chiller.

Pool Renovation vs New Build

If you already have a pool that needs updating, renovation is significantly cheaper:

Renovation TypeCost (AED)
Re-tiling only25,000 — 50,000
New filtration system8,000 — 20,000
Add pool chiller12,000 — 25,000
Complete renovation (retile + equipment + decking)50,000 — 100,000
Pro Tip: A pool chiller is not optional in Dubai. Without one, your pool water reaches 38-42°C in summer — unusable. Budget AED 12,000-25,000 for a chiller and AED 300-600 per month for its electricity running cost.

Timeline: How Long to Build a Pool?

A standard concrete pool takes 8 to 12 weeks from excavation to first swim. Fiberglass pools can be installed in 4-6 weeks. Infinity pools with custom engineering: 12-16 weeks.

Permits Required

  • Community NOC — Required from your developer (Emaar, Nakheel, DAMAC, etc.)
  • Dubai Municipality building permit — Required for structural excavation
  • DEWA connection — For pool pump and chiller electrical supply
  • Safety inspection — DM requires pool fencing and safety features
