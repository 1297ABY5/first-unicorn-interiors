What Makes DAMAC Hills Unique

DAMAC Hills is one of Dubai's most established master-planned communities, developed by DAMAC Properties around the Trump International Golf Club. With a mix of villas, townhouses and apartments set across manicured fairways and parklands, it has become home to thousands of families who value space, greenery and community living. The neighbouring DAMAC Hills 2 offers more affordable villa options while maintaining the same resort-style lifestyle, making both communities significant sources of renovation demand across Dubai.

Villas in DAMAC Hills range from compact three-bedroom townhouses to expansive six-bedroom estates, each with varying levels of developer-standard finishes. As many of these properties are now five to eight years old, homeowners are increasingly looking to refresh kitchens, bathrooms and living spaces that have started to show their age. The community's HOA, managed by DAMAC, requires approval for any external modifications including facade changes, garden structures, pool additions and boundary wall alterations. Our team has navigated these approval processes across 18 projects and maintains familiarity with the specific requirements and timelines involved.

The most popular upgrades in DAMAC Hills reflect the community's family-oriented character. Kitchen renovations dominate — homeowners want larger islands, better storage solutions and premium appliances to support the entertaining lifestyle that comes with villa living. Bathroom upgrades are a close second, with many residents replacing builder-grade sanitaryware and tiles with designer fixtures, walk-in showers and freestanding baths. Swimming pool construction remains consistently popular, particularly among villas with larger plots that face the golf course. Outdoor living — pergolas, barbecue areas, landscaping and lighting — rounds out the most requested services, as residents seek to maximise their garden spaces for year-round use.

What Happens at Each Stage

A typical full villa renovation, week by week. Bathroom and kitchen timelines are condensed versions of this same sequence.

Week 1-2 — Pre-Construction

Design Finalisation & Material Orders

Final design sign-off, material selections locked, NOC application submitted. This is when tile, marble, fixtures, and cabinetry are ordered — some materials have 3-4 week lead times, so delays here cascade through the entire project.

Week 3-4 — Demolition & Rough Works

Strip-Out & Structural Prep

Demolition of existing finishes, plumbing rough-in, electrical rewiring, waterproofing. The villa will look worse before it looks better — this is normal. Your project manager sends daily WhatsApp photo updates so nothing is a surprise.

Week 5-8 — Core Works

Tiling, Joinery & Glass Installation

Floor and wall tiling, kitchen and wardrobe installation, glass enclosures and partitions fitted. This is where the vision starts to come together. Each trade works in sequence — our in-house team means zero coordination delays between subcontractors.

Week 9-12 — Finishing

Paint, Fixtures & Detail Work

Painting, lighting installation, sanitaryware fitting, hardware, final glass polishing. Every detail is checked against the original design specification. Your dedicated project manager walks the site with the snagging list.

Week 13-14 — Handover

Deep Clean & Final Walkthrough

Professional deep clean, client walkthrough, snagging corrections (if any), handover documentation. You receive your 1-year craftsmanship warranty certificate and maintenance guide. Move-in ready.
