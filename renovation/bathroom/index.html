The Problem

Why Bathrooms Leak in Dubai

Bathroom leaks are the single most common renovation failure in Dubai. The reason is simple: most contractors treat waterproofing as an afterthought. They apply a thin coat of liquid membrane, skip the corners and joints, tile over it the same day, and move on. Three months later, water seeps through to the room below.

Dubai's extreme humidity makes the problem worse. Moisture finds every gap in poorly applied waterproofing. The result is damage to ceilings, walls, electrical systems and — in severe cases — structural elements. A bathroom renovation done cheaply today becomes an expensive repair tomorrow.

  • Poor waterproofing — single coat, no reinforcement at corners
  • Rushed work — tiling over uncured membrane
  • Cheap materials — thin tiles crack, poor grout deteriorates
  • No flood test before tiling — leaks discovered too late

Bathroom Renovation FAQ

A single bathroom renovation typically takes 3 to 5 weeks. This includes demolition, waterproofing (which requires curing time), plumbing rough-in, tiling, vanity installation, glass shower enclosure and final fixtures. If you are renovating multiple bathrooms simultaneously, we can often run them in parallel, completing 3-4 bathrooms within 5-6 weeks.
Dubai's climate creates unique challenges for bathrooms. High humidity accelerates moisture penetration through grout and tile joints. Many buildings in Dubai have experienced water damage because bathrooms were not properly waterproofed during original construction or renovation. We apply a multi-layer waterproofing system with liquid membrane, reinforcing fabric at all joints, and a flood test before any tiling begins. This prevents leaks to rooms below and protects the building structure from long-term moisture damage.
For Dubai bathrooms, we recommend large-format porcelain tiles (600x1200mm or 800x1600mm) or natural marble slabs for a seamless luxury look. Large tiles mean fewer grout lines, which reduces maintenance and creates a cleaner aesthetic. For shower floors, we use smaller format tiles or mosaics with anti-slip ratings for safety. We source tiles from premium Italian and Spanish manufacturers including Marazzi, Porcelanosa, and Florim.
Yes. We install electric underfloor heating mats beneath the tile finish. This is particularly popular in marble and porcelain bathrooms where the floor can feel cold, especially during winter months. The heating element is installed after waterproofing and before tile adhesive, with a thermostat control mounted on the wall. It adds approximately AED 3,000-5,000 per bathroom depending on size.
We design and fabricate custom vanities in our joinery workshop. Every vanity is built to the exact dimensions of your bathroom, with your choice of finish, countertop material and basin type. We use moisture-resistant marine-grade plywood for the carcass and apply waterproof finishes to all surfaces. This approach gives you a perfect fit and a premium look that ready-made units cannot match.
Our bathroom renovation pricing includes: complete demolition and disposal, waterproofing with flood test, plumbing rough-in and final connections, electrical work, floor and wall tiling, custom vanity with basin, mirror and accessories, frameless glass shower enclosure, toilet and bidet installation, and final grouting and silicone. Fixtures and fittings (taps, shower systems, toilets) are specified during the design stage and priced separately based on your selections from brands we work with.
