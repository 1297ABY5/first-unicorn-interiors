The Complete Guide to Bathroom Renovation in Dubai (2026)

A bathroom renovation is the single most impactful upgrade you can make to a Dubai villa. It is the room you start and end every day in — and the room that shows its age fastest in Dubai’s climate. Humidity, hard water, and daily use take a toll that makes bathrooms age faster here than almost anywhere else.

We have renovated over 300 bathrooms across Dubai. Here is everything you need to know before starting yours.

The One Thing You Cannot Skip: Waterproofing

This is the most critical part of any Dubai bathroom renovation. Waterproofing is not optional. It is the difference between a renovation that lasts 15 years and one that causes water damage within 18 months.

Proper waterproofing includes: membrane application to the entire wet area (floor and walls to 1.8m minimum), reinforcement at all corners and joints, drainage integration, and a 24-48 hour flood test before any tiling begins. We photograph and video the entire waterproofing process for every project. If your contractor does not do this, ask why.

Material Selection: What Works in Dubai

Flooring and Walls

Porcelain tiles (AED 80-200/sqm) are the most practical choice. Water-resistant, durable, and available in finishes replicating marble, concrete, and wood. Natural marble (AED 250-800/sqm) is the luxury standard — Calacatta, Statuario, and Carrara are most requested. Beautiful, but requires sealing and annual maintenance. Large format tiles (60x120cm or 80x160cm) are trending in 2026 — fewer grout lines means a cleaner look.

Fixtures and Fittings

Budget brands run AED 2,000-5,000 for a full set. Premium European brands (Grohe, Hansgrohe, Duravit) cost AED 8,000-15,000. Luxury options (Gessi, CEA Design) start at AED 20,000+. Brushed gold and matte black finishes dominate Dubai’s premium market in 2026.

Glass Enclosures

Frameless glass is standard in premium Dubai bathrooms. Our in-house glass team fabricates and installs custom enclosures with precise tolerances. Budget AED 3,000-8,000 for standard frameless, or AED 8,000-15,000 for walk-in configurations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Choosing tiles before confirming layout. Your tile pattern, drain position, and niche locations all depend on the final layout. Ignoring ventilation. Dubai’s humidity makes extraction fans critical. Underestimating storage. Niches, vanity drawers, and mirror cabinets should be designed in from the start.
