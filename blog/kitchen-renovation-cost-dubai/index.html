How Much Does a Kitchen Renovation Cost in Dubai? (2026 Guide)

The question every Dubai homeowner asks first: how much will it actually cost to renovate my kitchen? After completing over 800 projects across Dubai, we can give you a straight answer with real numbers.

The short version: a kitchen renovation in Dubai typically costs between AED 60,000 and AED 250,000+, depending on size, materials, and scope. Let us break it down properly.

What Drives the Cost Up (and Down)

Materials: Where Most of Your Budget Goes

Materials typically account for 55-65% of a kitchen renovation budget. The single biggest cost variable is your countertop and cabinetry choice. Engineered stone countertops start around AED 800 per linear metre. Natural marble like Calacatta can run AED 2,500+ per linear metre. Custom handleless cabinetry from European suppliers costs 2-3x more than locally manufactured options — but the quality difference is immediately visible.

Layout Changes: The Hidden Cost Multiplier

Keeping your existing kitchen layout can save 15-25% of the total cost. The moment you move plumbing or electrical points, costs increase significantly. Moving a sink or adding an island with plumbing requires re-routing pipes through the slab — concrete cutting, waterproofing, and Dubai Municipality compliance.

Appliance Integration

Built-in appliances cost more than freestanding but deliver a cleaner aesthetic. Budget AED 20,000-50,000 for a full integrated appliance package from brands like Siemens, Miele, or Gaggenau.

What Should Be in a Kitchen Renovation Quote

A proper quote should itemise: design and planning (3D visualisation, material selection), demolition and waste removal, electrical (lighting circuits, appliance points), plumbing (sink, dishwasher, filtration), cabinetry (custom manufactured, installed and aligned), countertops (templated, fabricated, installed), splashback, appliances (supply and installation), and finishing (paint, trim, handles, final clean).

If a quote does not break down each of these categories with specific brands and quantities, ask why. Vague “allowances” give the contractor room to substitute cheaper materials.

How to Save Without Sacrificing Quality

Keep the existing layout — saves AED 15,000-30,000. Choose engineered stone over natural marble for countertops — nearly identical look at 40% less cost. Go locally manufactured for cabinetry — UAE workshops produce excellent quality at a fraction of European prices. And time your renovation for summer when contractors have more availability.
