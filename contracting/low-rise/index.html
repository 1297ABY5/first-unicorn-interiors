The Challenge

Multi-Storey Construction Demands Developer-Level Experience

Building a low-rise structure is fundamentally different from constructing a villa. The structural loads are greater, the MEP systems are more complex, the authority requirements are stricter, and the financial exposure is significantly higher. A mistake at foundation level does not just affect one floor — it compromises the entire building.

Dubai's regulatory environment adds another layer of complexity. Low-rise buildings require approvals from multiple authorities simultaneously: municipality for structure, Civil Defence for fire safety, DEWA for power and water, RTA for access and parking, and the master developer for community compliance. Managing these parallel approval tracks whilst keeping construction on schedule requires a team that has done it many times before.

We have built low-rise residential and mixed-use buildings across multiple Dubai zones. Our experience means fewer surprises, faster approvals, and a building that performs for its investors long after completion.

Our Process

From Plot to Completion

1

Feasibility

Plot analysis, zoning review, soil investigation, and preliminary design. We confirm buildable area, permitted height, parking requirements, and estimated budget.

2

Design & Permit

Full architectural, structural, and MEP design. Authority submissions and building permit. All drawings prepared to municipality standards with coordinated BIM models.

3

Construction

Enabling works, piling (if required), structural frame, MEP installation, block work, plastering, waterproofing, finishing, and external works. Milestone-based payments throughout.

4

Handover

Final inspections, snagging, commissioning, utility connections, and certificate of completion. Building delivered ready for occupancy with full documentation package.
